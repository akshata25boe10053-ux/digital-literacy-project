# Case Study: UPI Payment Fraud

## Type of Cybercrime
UPI / Online Payment Fraud

## What is it?
UPI (Unified Payments Interface) fraud is a type of cybercrime where
scammers trick users into sending money or revealing their UPI PIN
through deception, fake links, or social engineering.

## How it Happens (Step by Step)
1. The victim receives a call/message from someone posing as a bank
   official, delivery agent, or KYC verification officer.
2. The scammer creates urgency — "Your account will be blocked" or
   "You have a pending delivery, pay Rs.1 to confirm."
3. The victim is sent a fake payment link or asked to scan a QR code.
4. The victim enters their UPI PIN thinking they are receiving money
   — but they are actually sending it.
5. Money is instantly transferred and is nearly impossible to recover.

## Who is Targeted?
- First-time UPI users (especially college students)
- Senior citizens unfamiliar with digital payments
- Online shoppers on platforms like OLX or Facebook Marketplace
- Anyone receiving a "prize" or "cashback" message

## Consequences
- Immediate financial loss (cases range from Rs.500 to lakhs)
- Mental stress and anxiety
- Loss of trust in digital payment systems
- In severe cases, victims take loans to recover losses
